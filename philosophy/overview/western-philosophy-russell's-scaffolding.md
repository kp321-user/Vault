Notes: 
- Thales, Pythagoras, Democritus rated **Foundational** despite no surviving works — their ideas (naturalism, mathematical reality, atomism) seeded entire traditions even if we only know them secondhand
- Berkeley rated **Notable** rather than Major — hugely original but his direct influence was narrow; he mostly served as the stepping stone Hume needed
- Bergson rated **Notable** — enormously fashionable in his time, largely eclipsed since, though he influenced Whitehead and process philosophy
- Anselm rated **Notable** — the ontological argument is famous but his overall influence is modest compared to Aquinas
- notes on the selection:

- **Islamic philosophy** (Avicenna, Averroes, Al-Ghazali) is rated Foundational because without them Aristotle's works wouldn't have survived into medieval Europe — they preserved and transmitted the texts Russell's entire Book Two depends on
- **Wittgenstein** is probably the single biggest omission from Russell's book given Russell's own tradition — their relationship was also famously difficult
- **Kierkegaard** is the continental counterweight to Hegel that Russell largely skips
- **de Beauvoir and Arendt** are 20th century figures whose influence has only grown since Russell wrote
