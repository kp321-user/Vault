Notes: 
- **Islamic philosophy** (Avicenna, Averroes, Al-Ghazali) is rated Foundational because without them Aristotle's works wouldn't have survived into medieval Europe — they preserved and transmitted the texts Russell's entire Book Two depends on
- **Wittgenstein** is probably the single biggest omission from Russell's book given Russell's own tradition — their relationship was also famously difficult
- **Kierkegaard** is the continental counterweight to Hegel that Russell largely skips
