# Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646–1716)

## In plain terms
brilliant scholar, mathematician, invented calculus
had a big metaphysical system
everything that is complex can be broken down into constituent components (identifies the term "Monad" as single indivisible substance). e.g. god, human souls are monads
monads cannot be material because, by definition
? matter is something extended, and extension is always subdivisible
monads cant be material and therefore something immaterial
truths can be truths of reason (theory) or truths of fact (need experience)
mind-body dualism (Descartes) challenged by stating that all matter is phenomenal at all levels 
therefore god does not have to comprise this additional false category of substance 
believed in god, uses the idea of god to explain the relations between monads (seems overly ambitious, likely related to rationalist goal)
tried to combine the separation of the material world and religion
