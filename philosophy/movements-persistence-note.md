## Key concepts so far

- **Long march through the institutions** — Rudi Dutschke (~1967), Gramscian roots (cultural hegemony, war of position), popularized by Marcuse. Valid as a description of strategy; weak as a grand explanatory theory (self-selection > coordinated infiltration). Federalist Society as the most successful executed example.
- **Interest-based vs identity-based movements** — interest movements dissolve when the grievance is addressed; identity movements persist because exit costs include community, self-conception, family.
- **Resistance to criticism** — epistemic closure (movement supplies the criteria for evidence); self-sealing logic (opposition predicted by doctrine, thus confirms it); sunk-cost commitment (Festinger, *When Prophecy Fails*); high entry costs improve retention.
- **How movements start** — grievances are common; the scarce ingredients are organizational infrastructure (churches → civil rights; universities → 68ers) and moments when repression falters or elites split. Tocqueville: revolutions erupt when conditions are *improving*.
- **Persistence = institutionalization** — converting energy into durable institutions. But see the **Weber–Michels thesis**: organizations develop self-preserving interests that displace the original mission (Occupy as refusal-to-institutionalize; civil rights as threading the needle).
- **Professionalized grievance** — movements sustained by livelihoods that depend on the grievance never being resolved.
