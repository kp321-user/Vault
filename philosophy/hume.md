## In plain terms
causality, skeptic

psychology, politics, morality should be discussed with factual background, concretely
Hume's fork: with any given body of ideas, do they concern matters of fact (bases on observation or experience) or do they concern the relations of ideas like mathematics and logic. If not, its rubbish.
He was also aware of the small amount of  human knowledge that can actually be proven, and was aware that even this knowledge was less imprtant than having a good understanding of human nature and what's involved in living a good life. Human nature is real and innate (not a blank slate at birth)
