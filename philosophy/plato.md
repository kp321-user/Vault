## Who Plato influenced
- Aristotle
- The Academy, his new university, Academic skepticism = Plato's school turned Socratic-skeptical (Arcesilaus, Carneades) → anti-Stoic argument that no impression guarantees truth → act on the _probabile_ without dogmatic assent → transmitted to the Latin world almost entirely through Cicero (_Academica_, _De Natura Deorum_, _De Divinatione_) → becomes Augustine's post-Manichee refuge → refuted in _Contra Academicos_ via self-certainty of the doubting mind → resurfaces in Descartes. It's one of the cleanest single threads running from Athens through Rome to modernity.
