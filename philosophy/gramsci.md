# Antonio Francesco Gramsci (1891 – 1937)

## In plain terms
- co-founded the Italian Communist Party and served as its leader until his arrest in 1926
- best known for developing the theory of cultural hegemony, which explains how ruling classes maintain power through cultural institutions and ideological consent rather than just force.  His work significantly influenced critical theory, sociology, and political science by analyzing the relationship between the state, civil society, and the role of intellectuals in challenging capitalist dominance.
- social epistemology
- political philosophy 
- cultural hegemony is a normative-theoretical framework, not an empirical finding
