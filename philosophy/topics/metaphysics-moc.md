# Metaphysics — Map of Content

The question: what is fundamentally real?

## Substance traditions (reality as things)
- [[aristotle]] — form and matter; substance as primary
- [[descartes]] — mind and body as distinct substances
- [[leibniz]] — monads as fundamental units

## Process traditions (reality as becoming)
- [[heraclitus]] — constant flux; nothing static
- [[whitehead]] — occasions of experience; process over substance
- [[bergson]] — time as lived duration

## Idealist traditions (reality as mind/idea)
- [[plato]] — Forms as more real than physical things
- [[kant]] — reality shaped by the structure of mind
- [[hegel]] — Absolute Spirit unfolding through history

## Buddhist/Eastern
- [[nagarjuna]] — emptiness; nothing has inherent existence
- [[laozi]] — the Tao as unnameable ground of being
