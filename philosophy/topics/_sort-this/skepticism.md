Academic skepticism doesn't just flow forward into Augustine; it also flows into Sextus Empiricus and the Pyrrhonist line, which goes dormant for a millennium, gets rediscovered in the 1560s (Sextus's texts printed), feeds Montaigne, and _that_ branch — not the Augustinian one — is what Descartes is actually responding to. Meanwhile Descartes' _cogito_ has two parents: the Montaigne-skeptical crisis _and_ Augustine's _si fallor, sum_. And downstream, the response to Cartesian doubt splits at least three ways: rationalist (Spinoza), empiricist (Hume radicalizes the doubt), and pragmatist (Peirce rejects the whole exercise — "let us not doubt in philosophy what we do not doubt in our hearts"). Chain-telling picks one path through that graph; the graph is the truth.
