
## John Dewey - 1890s and early 1900s

- Dewey’s educational ideas were being developed and tested by the **1890s**; he founded the University of Chicago’s Laboratory School in **1896** to experiment with his ideas in practice.[academia](https://www.academia.edu/42016209/Paulo_Freire_and_John_Dewey_dun_dialogue_impossible_%C3%A0_un_conflit_constructif_)
- Some of his major statements appeared later in books and lectures: _The School and Society_ (**1899**), _How We Think_ (**1910**), _Democracy and Education_ (**1916**), and _Experience and Education_ (**1938**).[academia](https://www.academia.edu/42016209/Paulo_Freire_and_John_Dewey_dun_dialogue_impossible_%C3%A0_un_conflit_constructif_)
- **Format:** mostly **books, essays, and lectures**, plus school experiments and public intellectual writing

## Paulo Freire - 1960s and early 1970s

- Freire’s best-known educational views were developed in the **1960s**, especially during his literacy work and political context in Brazil.[acjol](https://acjol.org/index.php/NJP/article/view/421)
- His most famous statement of those views is _Pedagogy of the Oppressed_, first published in **1968**.[acjol](https://acjol.org/index.php/NJP/article/view/421)
- **Format:** mainly **books**, but also **adult-literacy practice, dialogue-based educational work, and public talks/discussions** connected to popular education and critical pedagogy.
## Dewey timeline

John Dewey began publishing his educational views in the **1890s**, and he developed them across **books, essays, lectures, and school experiments**.marxists+1

- **1896** — _My Pedagogic Creed_ and _The Reflex Arc Concept in Psychology_; these are early statement pieces in **essay/article** form.[marxists](https://www.marxists.org/archive/dewey/index.htm)
    
- **1896** — The University of Chicago Laboratory School began, which was Dewey’s **practical experimental format** for testing his ideas.[brocku](https://brocku.ca/MeadProject/Dewey/timeline.html)
    
- **1899** — _The School and Society_; **book/lecture-based writing**.[marxists](https://www.marxists.org/archive/dewey/index.htm)
    
- **1902** — _The Child and the Curriculum_; **booklet/essay** form.[marxists](https://www.marxists.org/archive/dewey/index.htm)
    
- **1916** — _Democracy and Education_; major **book** statement of his philosophy.[marxists](https://www.marxists.org/archive/dewey/index.htm)
    
- **1938** — _Experience and Education_ and related essays like _The Need of a Theory of Experience_; **book/essay** form.[marxists](https://www.marxists.org/archive/dewey/index.htm)

## Freire timeline

Paulo Freire’s most influential educational views appear mainly in the **1960s and early 1970s**, especially through **books and literacy work**.bloomsbury+1

- **1963–1964** — Freire’s literacy and popular education work in Brazil became the practical setting for his ideas; this is the main **practice-based** format behind his theory.[news.educ.cam.ac](https://news.educ.cam.ac.uk/paulo-freire-100)
    
- **1968** — _Pedagogy of the Oppressed_ was first published in Portuguese; this is his key **book**.[bloomsbury](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/pedagogy-of-the-oppressed-9781501314131/)
    
- **1970** — the English translation of _Pedagogy of the Oppressed_ appeared.[bloomsbury](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/pedagogy-of-the-oppressed-9781501314131/)
    
- **1970s onward** — Freire continued developing his pedagogy through books, speeches, and teaching practice in exile and later public education work.[news.educ.cam.ac](https://news.educ.cam.ac.uk/paulo-freire-100)
